RBA Hikes Cash Rate to 4.35% Amid Inflation Surge

It is revealed that the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) is making significant adjustments to its monetary policy in response to higher-than-expected inflation. Following a robust Q3 2023 Consumer Price Index (CPI) report, the RBA is now expected to implement a 25 basis point rate hike in November, pushing the cash rate to 4.35%. This move is seen as an attempt to combat rising inflation and put monetary policy into a more restrictive stance. They anticipates that the cash rate will remain at this elevated level until at least Q3 2024, and the monetary policy may not return to a more neutral setting until 2025. However, various economic uncertainties, such as population growth and fiscal policies, add complexity to this outlook, and the balance of risks is being closely monitored.


The adjustments in monetary policy are largely driven by strong price increases in components of the CPI basket like petrol, rent, and utilities, which are beyond the RBA's control. This situation reflects a supply and demand imbalance, particularly in the housing market, where residential construction has not kept up with population growth, resulting in record-low vacancy rates and higher rents. Despite the expectations of further rate cuts in late 2024 and early 2025, monetary policy is expected to remain in restrictive territory, according to former RBA Governor Lowe's remarks, until 2025.




The article also highlights that there are risks on the horizon, including the possibility of a higher terminal cash rate than 4.35%, as well as concerns about the lagged impact of interest rate increases on consumers in 2024. In addition, population growth, fiscal policies, and geopolitical uncertainties can influence the path of inflation and interest rates. Therefore, close attention is being paid to key economic indicators, such as consumer spending, the labor market, prices, and wages, to guide future monetary policy decisions. The RBA's actions and outlook will continue to be closely monitored in the coming months.
