Top 10 Tips for Real Estate Sellers and Buyers in Western Australia


For Sellers:

  1. Understand the Market: Research the local property market to understand current trends, demand, and pricing. This will help you set a competitive price for your property.
  2. Hire a Skilled Real Estate Agent: Choose a real estate agent with a proven track record in your area. They can provide valuable insights and handle negotiations on your behalf.
  3. Present Your Property Well: First impressions matter. Invest in minor repairs, declutter, and consider professional staging to showcase your property in the best light.
  4. Set a Realistic Price: Overpricing can deter potential buyers. Set a realistic price based on market research and advice from your agent.
  5. Market Your Property Effectively: Utilize both online and offline channels to reach a broad audience. High-quality photos and virtual tours can attract more buyers.

For Buyers:

  1. Get Pre-Approved for a Loan Secure a pre-approval from your lender before house hunting. This shows sellers you’re a serious buyer and helps you understand your budget.
  2. Research the Area: Investigate the neighborhood, including amenities, schools, and future developments. Understanding the area helps you make an informed decision.
  3. Attend Open Houses: Visit multiple properties to get a feel for what’s available in your price range. Open houses are also an opportunity to ask questions and assess the property’s condition.
  4. Consider Future Growth: Look for properties in areas with potential for growth. This can increase the value of your investment over time.
  5. Negotiate Wisely: Don’t be afraid to negotiate on price, especially if the property has been on the market for a while. Your agent can help you with this process.
