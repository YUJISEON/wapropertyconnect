Dealing with home buyers
who won't tell me their budget or where they want to live can be tricky. When people contact me about buying a house, they often get excited. But when I ask how much money they have to spend and where they'd like to live, some of them get cagey.

It's understandable that people want to keep their financial and personal information private. However, knowing these things is crucial for finding them the right home. Without a clear idea of their budget, I might show them houses that are way too expensive or not up to their standards. And without knowing their preferred location, I can't narrow down the search to areas they'd like.

The trouble begins when I show them homes that don't meet their secret criteria. They get frustrated because they're not finding what they want, and I'm frustrated because it's like trying to hit a moving target.

To solve this, we need to talk openly and build trust. I have to make clients feel okay about sharing their budget and where they want to live. It's not about invading their privacy; it's about helping them find the perfect home. And I need to listen carefully to their concerns and address them. That way, we can work together to make their house-hunting journey smoother and more successful.

What I say,
Nothing else much, just tell me what you are looking for. I will try my best to find a home of your dream.
